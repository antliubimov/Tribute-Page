Nelson Mandela

A symbol of global peacemaking

Nelson Mandela became the first black president of South Africa in 1994, serving until 1999. He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.
  • 1918 18 July - Mandela was born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa's Cape Province.
  • 1927 - When Mandela was about nine, Mandela's mother took him to the "Great Place" palace at Mqhekezweni, where he was entrusted to the guardianship of the Thembu regent, Chief Jongintaba Dalindyebo.
  • 1934 - Aged 16, he, Justice and several other boys travelled to Tyhalarha to undergo the ulwaluko circumcision ritual that symbolically marked their transition from boys to men; afterwards he was given the name Dalibunga.
  • 1933 - Mandela began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo, a Western-style institution that was the largest school for black Africans in Thembuland.
  • 1937 - He completed his Junior Certificate in two years, and moved to Healdtown, the Methodist college in Fort Beaufort attended by most Thembu royalty, including Justice.
  • 1939 - Mandela began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare, an elite black institution in Alice, Eastern Cape, with around 150 students. There he studied English, anthropology, politics, native administration, and Roman Dutch law in his first year, desiring to become an interpreter or clerk in the Native Affairs Department.
  • 1950 March - Mandela took Xuma's place on the ANC national executive, and that same year was elected national president of the ANCYL
  • 1952 April - Mandela began work at the H.M. Basner law firm, which was owned by a communist, although his increasing commitment to work and activism meant he spent less time with his family.
  • 1952 July - Mandela was arrested under the Suppression of Communism Act and stood trial as one of the 21 accused—among them Moroka, Sisulu, and Yusuf Dadoo—in Johannesburg.
  • 1956 - Along with 155 others, Mandela is accused of conspiring to overthrow the South African state by violent means and is charged with treason. After a four-year trial, the charges are dropped
  • 1960 - March 21 People in Sharpeville protest against restrictions on their freedom of movement. Police kill 69 people, including women and children, and a state of emergency is declared amid fears of retaliation. Later the same month, the ANC is banned, a state of emergency is imposed and Mandela is detained
  • 1963 7 August - The Security Council adopted Resolution 181 calling upon all states to cease the sale and shipment of arms, ammunition and military vehicles to South Africa. The arms embargo was made mandatory on November 4 1977
  • 1964 12 June - Mandela and seven others are sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage on Robben Island, off Cape Town
  • 1985 January - President PW Botha offers Mandela conditional release from prison, which Mandela refuses. Later that year, Mandela initiates talks with the apartheid regime when he writes to Kobie Coetsee, minister of justice
  • 1990 11 February - Mandela is released from prison, greeted by large crowds. That year, negotiations begin about the formation of a multiracial democracy in South Africa. Nelson Mandela spent 27 years in prison, from November 1962 until February 1990.
  • 1993 - Mandela, along with South African president FW de Klerk, is jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for efforts to bring stability to South Africa. Accepting the award, Mandela says: “We live with the hope that as she battles to remake herself, South Africa will be like a microcosm of the new world that is striving to be born.”
  • 1994 10 May - Inaugurated as first black president of a democratic South Africa. De Klerk becomes vice-president in his Government of National Unity. Thabo Mbeki, Mandela’s deputy, takes over the day-to-day running of government, leaving Mandela free to promote the country around the world
  • 1999 - Steps down after one term as president; hands power to Mbeki who goes on to become president after the ANC wins the 1999 presidential election.
  • 2004 June Mandela announces that he will be retiring from public life.
  • 2007 18 July - Mandela and wife Graca Machel co-founded The Elders, a group of world leaders aiming to work both publicly and privately to find solutions to some of the world's toughest issues.
  • 2013 June - Mandela is admitted to hospital with lung infection
  • 2013 5 December - Nelson Mandela died.

"Today the world has lost one of its most important leaders and one of its finest human beings. History will remember Nelson Mandela as a champion for human dignity and freedom, for peace and reconciliation."
